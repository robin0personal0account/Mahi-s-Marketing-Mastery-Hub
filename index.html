Info Cards

What is a Market?

A place where parties exchange goods and services. 🏪 Physical: Retail outlets (face-to-face). 🌐 Virtual: Online platforms (no contact).

What is Marketing?

Dr. Philip Kotler: “The science and art of exploring, creating, and delivering value to satisfy needs at a profit.” 📈 Involves research 🔍, promotion 📣, selling 💰, distribution 🚚.

Needs, Wants, Demand

💡 Needs: Essentials (e.g., food). 🌟 Wants: Desired items (e.g., phone). 💸 Demand: Willingness and ability to buy.

Marketing Concept

Focus: Customer satisfaction. Strategy: Pull (e.g., Amazon’s personalized recommendations 🚚).

Societal Marketing

Balances customer satisfaction, profits, and societal well-being 🌍 (e.g., eco-friendly brands).

Product Concept

Focus: Quality and innovation (e.g., Apple’s iPhone 📱—premium and cutting-edge).

Production Concept

Affordable, accessible products (e.g., Walmart’s low prices 💵 via bulk buying).

Selling Concept

Push strategy—aggressive sales (e.g., insurance firms 📞).

Q&A Study Notes

What’s a market?

A place for exchanging goods/services. 🏪 Physical: Stores. 🌐 Virtual: Online (e.g., eBay).

Define marketing (Kotler).

“Science and art of delivering value to satisfy needs profitably.” 📈 Includes research 🔍, promotion 📣, sales 💰, distribution 🚚.

What are Needs, Wants, Demand?

💡 Needs: Survival essentials (water). 🌟 Wants: Desires (new shoes). 💸 Demand: Ability to buy (e.g., wanting and affording a latte).

What’s the Marketing Concept?

Focus on customer satisfaction with a pull strategy. E.g., Amazon’s fast shipping 🚚 and personalization.

Explain the Societal Marketing Concept.

Balances customer needs, profits, and society 🌍. E.g., Patagonia’s eco-friendly gear ♻️.

What’s the Product Concept?

Focus on quality and innovation. E.g., Apple’s sleek iPhones 📱.

What’s the Production Concept?

Affordable, accessible products via efficiency. E.g., Walmart’s bulk buying for low prices 💵.

What’s the Selling Concept?

Push strategy—aggressive sales. E.g., telemarketers pushing unwanted products 📞.

Why is marketing significant?

Creates value 🎁, builds relationships 🤝, drives growth 📊.

What’s Step 1 of the marketing process?

Mission: Define purpose. E.g., Google: “Organize info universally” 🌐.

What’s Step 2: Situation Analysis?

Assess internal (strengths, resources) and external (competition, trends) factors 🔍.

What’s Step 3: Marketing Strategy?

Define target audience 🎯, set measurable goals 📏, plan budget 💰.

What’s Step 4: Marketing Mix?

7 P’s: Product 🎁, Price 💵, Place 🏪, Promotion 📣, People 👥, Process ⚙️, Physical Evidence 🏬.

What’s Step 5: Implementation & Control?

Launch plans 🚀, monitor with benchmarks 📅 (e.g., monthly goals).

What’s SWOT analysis?

Strengths 💪, Weaknesses ⚠️, Opportunities 🌟, Threats ⚡ (e.g., Walmart).

What’s PEST analysis?

Political 🏛️, Economic 💰, Social 👥, Technological 💻.

What’s the business environment?

Micro 🔍: Nearby factors (customers). Macro 🌍: Big forces (economy).

What’s a target audience?

A specific group your product fits best (e.g., teens for trendy sneakers 👟).

What’s a marketing budget?

Costs for ads 📺, content 📱, branding 🎨, PR 📢, staff 👥.

What’s micro environment?

Small factors: Customers 👥, suppliers 🚛, competitors 🏅.

What’s macro environment?

Big forces: Economy 💰, politics 🏛️, tech 💻, trends 👗.

Real-World Examples

Insurance Firms (Selling Concept)

Aggressive sales tactics 📞 to push policies, even if unneeded.
